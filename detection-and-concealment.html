Frontline Commander  //  Field Manual 04

Detection
& Concealment Seeing is not spotting.

A unit can be in plain view and still go unnoticed. Sight range gets you a look; concealment decides whether that look becomes a contact. Learn the fog and you'll win engagements before they start.

01

The Three Checks

Every tick, for every enemy near your forces, the game runs three gates in order. All three must pass before that enemy appears on your screen — model and icon. Fail any one and they melt back into the fog.

1

Line of Sight

Can one of your units draw an unblocked line to it? Hills, buildings and treelines physically block the view. No clear line means invisible — at any range, no exceptions.

Gate — geometry · terrain · hard cover
2

Detection Range

Is it close enough? Your full sight range is cut down by how well the target hides. A concealed enemy has to be far closer before you can pick it out.

Gate — signature · cover · exposure · optics
3

Spot Time

Have you watched long enough? In the open, that's instant. In cover it takes seconds of unbroken observation — and if the target slips out of view, the clock resets.

Gate — cover-driven spot delay
02

How Range Is Cut Down

Detection range starts at your unit's sight range and gets multiplied by five factors. Two shrink it — that's concealment. Two grow it. It never exceeds your base sight range.

03

Signature — The Platform

Signature is how observable a unit is by its very nature — size, noise, dust, heat. A tank is a big loud target you can't help but notice; infantry keep low and quiet. It's a flat multiplier on the range at which that unit can be detected: 1.0 = fully visible, lower = stealthier.

Recon 0.60

04

Cover — The Terrain

Where a unit stands stacks on top of its signature. Forests smother detection; open fields offer nothing. And the strongest concealment of all isn't in this table — it's hard cover: put a hill or building between you and the enemy and you break Line of Sight entirely. That's Check 1 — invisible at any range.

TerrainIn-game surfaceCoverRange kept
Open ground Grass, fields, roads 100%
Light forest Leafy grass, scattered trees 58%
Dense forest Deep woods (the no-vehicle mud) 15%
Hard cover Hills, buildings — blocks the line 0%

06

Spot Time — The Clock

Being in range isn't the same as being seen yet. Cover doesn't just shrink range — it makes a contact take time to resolve. Watch an unbroken sightline and a hidden unit fades in; look away, or let it duck behind terrain, and the progress bleeds off.

Where the target sitsTime to spotFeel
Open ground~instantYou see it the moment it's in range.
Light forest~1 – 2 secA beat of doubt before the contact firms up.
Dense forest~2 – 4 secSlowest at the edge of range; quicker up close.

// Moving targets resolve faster. And breaking line of sight resets the timer — a unit that keeps popping in and out of cover is a nightmare to pin.

07

What Gives You Away

+50% On the move

A moving unit is easier to catch — motion widens the range it's detected at and speeds up the spot clock. You can't sneak at a run. A still ambush in cover is far quieter than one repositioning.

Instant Opening fire

The instant a unit fires, the muzzle flash reveals it at full sight range, immediately — cover and signature stop mattering. Fire from concealment and you're committed. Volley, then reposition.

Your eyes — Recon & optics

Optics is the one factor working for the watcher. Recon units carry stronger optics, so they punch through concealment — spotting hidden enemies sooner and farther than a tank ever could. Screen your advance with them and you'll see the ambush before you walk into it.

08

Field Tactics

A1

Infantry own the woods. ~5% detection in dense forest makes them the premier ambushers — invisible until an enemy is right on top of them.

A2

Tanks can't hide in the open. 100% signature means they're seen from max range. Mask armour behind terrain and move it through cover, not across fields.

A3

Terrain beats camouflage. Breaking line of sight with a hill or building is total concealment — no detection at any range. Use ridgelines and buildings as walls.

A4

Let recon lead. Their optics spot first. Push scouts ahead of your main force so you're the one who opens the engagement.

A5

Hold fire until you mean it. The first shot lights you up at full range. Ambush with everything at once, then relocate before the answer comes.

A6

Respect the treeline. You won't see what's inside a wood until you're close — and by then their infantry already see you. Never advance blind into forest.

A7

Stillness is stealth. Movement raises your profile and quickens the enemy's spot clock. Set your ambush and wait.
