TTK Doctrine
& Tier Law Feel first. Stats derived.

Stop tuning individual numbers. Define how every fight should feel — in shots and seconds — then derive every stat from that contract, and make the three-tier era system a single law that can never break it.

01

Where realism lives, where fun lives

The core trick — Warno does exactly this: realism goes in the behavior, fun goes in the numbers.

Realism The behavior layer

Lock-on delays, facing arcs, LoS and cover, deploy times, reload rhythms, scatter, missile flight time, refuse-wrong-target logic. This is where the game feels authentic — and none of it can one-shot anyone.

Fun The numbers layer

HP pools and damage are pure game design. A real Javelin kills any real tank in one hit; ours does 60% and cripples, because a fight you can react to is a fight worth having. The rhythm is real; the lethality is tuned.

The six floor rules never violated — any tier, any matchup

F1

No same-tier one-shots on vehicles

With exactly one sanctioned exception: an ATGM into the side or rear. That's the "you got flanked" punishment — telegraphed (3s lock + missile flight ≈ 4.7s of warning) and rationed (pop cost, long reload).

F2

Every kill has ≥ 3 seconds of counterplay

From first warning (spotted chatter, lock-on, first hit) to death. If a unit dies with no decision available to its owner, the numbers are wrong.

F3

Small arms never delete

Infantry-vs-infantry is attrition (15–20s), never burst removal. Squads lose soldiers one at a time — the fight visibly degrades, it doesn't evaporate.

F4

Cross-tier dominance may break F1

A T3 shell one-shotting a T1 scout is the reward for teching. Dominance down the ladder is the fantasy; fragility up the ladder is the price of massing cheap units.

F5

No free air

Air power never trades HP on the line — it trades exposure against the AA umbrella. A heli inside same-tier AA range dies in seconds; a jet entering it takes heavy damage or aborts. If air can strike without risking anything, AA has failed as a role.

F6

Every strike is telegraphed

F2 applied to the sky: a jet run announces itself (engine audio + target marker) 3–4s before ordnance lands; a heli's ATGM has the same lock-on beat as the infantry Javelin. Nobody dies to an invisible plane.

03

The air & AA contract

Two different kinds of air. Helicopters are persistent units — they live on the map, hover, ambush, and die like units. Jets are call-in strikes — they enter from the map edge, deliver, and leave; their balance levers are cooldown, telegraph, and AA denial, never HP trading. AA is the tax every army pays once the sky opens at Tier 2.

LOOP

The air triangle. Helis hard-counter tanks (flying Javelins at 3× ground speed) → AA hard-counters helis (seconds, not a fight) → bombs and artillery hard-counter AA (soft, expensive, must sit near the front). Jets loop over the top: bombers delete AA blobs, the A-10 punishes light-vehicle spam, AA jets police the sky itself.

// why jets are call-ins, not units
Persistent jet circling the map  →  breaks F2 (no counterplay window) and turns AA into a permanent stalemate.
Strike run  →  you see the telegraph, you get your 3–4s, the AA umbrella made the run suicidal or it didn't — and the jet is gone either way. All the fantasy, none of the degenerate hover.
Helis stay units  →  precisely because ground autocannons + AA can fight them continuously.
04

The tier law

One rule generates every tier. Because HP and damage scale identically, the same-tier contract holds at every tier automatically — and cross-tier dominance emerges from the math instead of being tuned.

Invariant The diagonal is the contract

Every same-tier duel is a 4-shot fight, at every tier — the game always feels the same to two equal players. Off the diagonal: one tier up kills in 2–3 and takes 6+; two tiers up is a massacre. That's "recruit for the now vs research for the future," expressed as pure math — plus the T3 outranges and outspots by ~20%, so it usually lands the first two shells before the T1 replies.

Decided Cross-tier one-shots allowed

A T3 ATGM one-shots a T1 tank even frontally (630 vs 500 HP). F4 says that's the point — approved, ship without a clamp, playtest. If it ever feels bad, the fix is one Luau clamp in DamageSystem: no single hit exceeds 90% of MaxHealth on a full-health vehicle.
