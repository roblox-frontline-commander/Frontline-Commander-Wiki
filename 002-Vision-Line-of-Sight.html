Frontline Commander  //  Field Manual 002

Vision &
Line of Sight What your units can see.

Before you can detect an enemy, one of your units has to physically see it. Vision is the first gate — a literal line from a crew's eyes to the target, cut by every hill, wall and treeline in between. Own the sightlines and you fight from the dark.

01

Sight Is Shared

Every unit carries its own eyes — and pools what it sees with the rest of your side. This is the gate that comes before everything else in the fog of war.

Team One spotter, whole army

Vision is shared across your side. What a single scout on a ridge can see, your entire force sees — every gun can act on it. You don't need eyes on a target with the unit that fires at it.

Fog No line, no contact

An enemy that no friendly unit can see is completely hidden — no model, no icon, nothing. Only once a unit has an unbroken line to it does the detection clock even begin.

Next gate A clear line gets you a look. Whether that look becomes a contact — instantly, or after seconds of watching — is covered in Field Manual 001: Detection & Concealment.
02

The Sightline

Vision is cast as a straight line from the observing unit's eyepoint to the target. If anything solid sits on that line, the view is blocked — no partial credit. Some of the battlefield stops a sightline; some of it doesn't.

On the sightlineEffectWhy
Hills & ridgesBlocksTerrain is solid to sight — the ground itself masks everything behind it.
Buildings & structuresBlocksA wall between you and the target cuts the line like any hill.
Woods (trunks)BlocksWhere trunks actually intervene, the line is broken — the deeper the wood, the more it blinds.
Other unitsPassesFriend or foe, units never mask each other — you can always see past your own line.
Build pads & markersPassesThe invisible ground helpers under bases are ignored by sight.
03

Eye Height & Cresting Hills

Here's the mechanic that makes terrain matter. Units don't see from the ground, and they don't float above it — they look out from roughly turret or head height. So a hill or ridge that rises between you and a target climbs above your sightline and cuts it. You cannot see through dead ground, even at point-blank range.

Elevation flips it. Put that same unit on the hilltop and it looks straight down over the crest — sightline clear.

Two consequences fall out of this. High ground wins: a unit up top sees down over cover and crests that blind everyone below. And reverse-slope works: a unit tucked just behind a ridge is blind to what's beyond it — but is equally invisible to it. Sit hull-down behind a crest, expose only your gun, and you choose when the sightline opens.

04

Where They're Looking

A unit doesn't see equally in all directions. It sees farthest straight ahead, less to the flanks, and shortest to the rear — plus a tight, always-on 360° bubble for anything that gets close. Facing is a real stat: an enemy creeping up on a unit's flank or back is seen far later than one walking up its front.

05

Then Comes Range

A clear, well-aimed sightline still only reveals a target that's within reach. Every unit has a sight range — and how far of it actually applies shrinks the better the target is hidden (dense forest, low signature). Line of sight decides whether you could see it; range and concealment decide how far off and how fast you actually do.

06

Field Tactics

V1

Vision is a team sport. One scout on high ground lights up the enemy for your whole firing line. Buy eyes, not just guns.

V2

Take the high ground. Elevation sees down over crests and cover that blind everyone below — and gets its sightlines first.

V3

Fight hull-down. Sit a unit just behind a ridge, roll up to fire, fall back to hide. You control when the line opens — and expose almost nothing.

V4

Keep your front to the threat. Vision is longest forward and weakest to the rear. Angle your advance and guard your own flanks and back.

V5

Flank to stay unseen. Approach from a unit's side or rear and you're spotted far later — long enough to close the distance or line up the shot.

V6

Terrain is a wall, both ways. Hills, buildings and woods block sight in and out. Route your moves behind them to arrive unseen.

V7

Break the line to vanish. Slip behind a ridge or into a wood and the enemy loses the sightline — and the sighting. Then reappear where they aren't looking.
